## Tips for Selecting the Right Products

Drawing from our testing experience, here are some pointers to guide your choice:

- **Monitors**: Aim for at least a 1440p resolution if you want crisp text and images. We recommend IPS panels (known for wide viewing angles and accurate color) for office or creative work, since they keep the picture consistent even when you're not looking at the screen straight on.
- **Mice**: Prioritize comfort and shape over extreme DPI specs (DPI refers to cursor sensitivity). A mouse that fits your hand well will serve you better during long sessions. In our trials, models that accommodate different grip styles (palm, claw, or fingertip) stayed comfortable even after hours of use.
- **Keyboards**: Go with a mechanical keyboard if you want tactile feedback for heavy typing, or stick with a quiet membrane keyboard if noise is a concern. We tend to favor models with programmable keys for custom shortcuts or macros, as these features can streamline your workflow.
- **Value Tip**: Keep an eye on deals. During our research, we saw several quality products go on sale for 20–30% off (often on major sites like Amazon). Catching a good discount can let you afford an upgrade that might otherwise be out of reach.
- **Future Considerations**: Think ahead about compatibility and longevity. Devices with modern features like USB-C ports and modular components are becoming standard, which means easier connectivity and potential upgrades down the line. Choosing gear with these future-proof features can extend the life of your setup.
