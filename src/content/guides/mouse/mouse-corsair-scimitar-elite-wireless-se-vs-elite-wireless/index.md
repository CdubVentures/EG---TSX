**What the table means in day-to-day use**

- The **SE** is the “same feel, fewer compromises” option: you’re not buying a new shape—you’re buying better efficiency, *way* longer Bluetooth endurance, and Stream Deck workflows that can replace a lot of alt-tabbing.
- The original **Elite Wireless** is mostly a **price play** now. When it’s meaningfully cheaper, it’s still the easiest way to get the Scimitar side grid—without paying for the SE’s newest integrations.


## Which one should you buy?

**Pick the Scimitar Elite Wireless SE if you:**

- Want the newest Scimitar with the **Marksman S 33K** sensor and the most “future-proof” version of the platform.
- Use Bluetooth a lot (laptop + desktop, docking, couch PC) and want **“charge it rarely”** battery behavior.
- Stream, record, or run a lot of shortcuts—and like the idea of your MMO side grid doubling as a **mini Stream Deck**.
- Are choosing between the two at similar prices.

**Pick the Scimitar Elite Wireless (original) if you:**

- See it at a meaningful discount versus the SE.
- Don’t care about Stream Deck / Virtual Stream Deck workflows and just want **MMO binds + iCUE macros**.
- Want the cleanest, simplest version of the Scimitar idea without paying for the SE’s workflow features.

If the price gap is small, go SE. The older Elite Wireless mostly makes sense as a deal mouse now.

## Design, shape & comfort

These two mice feel the same in hand, because they’re the same physical platform where it matters:

- Right-handed ergonomic MMO shell with a pinky rest
- A tall, supportive hump and wide body that favors **palm** or **relaxed claw**
- 16 programmable buttons, including the adjustable **12-button Key Slider™**
- A “macro platform” weight class (~114 g), not an ultralight flick mouse

The Key Slider side panel is the secret sauce. You can slide the 12-button grid forward/back so the rows line up with your thumb length. That’s what makes the Scimitar layout feel learnable instead of cramped.

If you’ve used Naga-style MMO mice before, both Scimitars will feel immediately familiar. If you’re coming from a 60–70 g FPS mouse, both will feel like tanks—but that’s the point: comfort + button real estate over pure flick speed.

## Back to the comparison

Now that you’ve seen the live deals + spec snapshots for each mouse, here’s the part that usually decides it in real use: how they behave in games, how often you’ll charge them, and whether Stream Deck workflows actually change your setup.

### Sensor, latency & in-game performance

For MMO / ARPG / MOBA play, both mice are “more than enough”:

- Tracking is clean at practical DPI settings.
- Click response is fast thanks to optical main switches.
- In real MMO play, the bigger limiter is usually **thumb accuracy on the grid** and **muscle memory**, not sensor ceilings.

Where the SE pulls ahead is mostly about **efficiency + headroom** (and the way that ties into battery behavior), not “it feels dramatically faster.”

### Wireless modes & battery life

Both support:

- 2.4 GHz **Slipstream** via USB receiver
- **Bluetooth** for laptops / secondary devices
- **USB-C wired** for charging and play-while-charging

The separator is Bluetooth endurance:

- **SE:** built to behave like a “weeks at a time” mouse in Bluetooth use with RGB off.
- **Original:** still strong, but much more “normal” for a wireless gaming mouse.

If you use Bluetooth often, the SE’s advantage is the kind you feel constantly. If you’re always on Slipstream, battery becomes less of a deciding factor—and price matters more.

### Software, macros & Stream Deck workflows

Both mice work in **Corsair iCUE** for:

- Full remapping of all 16 buttons
- Macros and multi-action commands
- Profiles per game/app
- RGB control (logo + side panel)

**Only the SE** adds native Stream Deck / Virtual Stream Deck support, which changes what the side grid can do:

- Not just “keybinds,” but actual Stream Deck actions and multi-step workflows
- Scenes, audio routing, mute states, VOD markers, app shortcuts, and more
- Multiple “layers” of controls without needing another device on your desk

If you never stream or automate workflows, this may feel like a bonus feature. If you do, it’s the single biggest reason the SE exists.

### Who each mouse is best for

**MMO, MOBA & ARPG grinders**
- **SE:** best if you want one mouse that can also run your stream / comms / shortcuts cleanly.
- **Original:** best if you want the Scimitar grid for less money.

**Mixed MMO + FPS**
- Both are heavy MMO shapes first. If FPS is your main game, you’ll usually be happier with a dedicated lightweight FPS mouse.
- Between these two, choose based on battery + workflows (SE) versus price (original).

**Productivity & multi-device setups**
- **SE:** the clear winner if you treat your MMO mouse as your daily driver.
- **Original:** fine if you mostly live on one PC and just want a deal.

**Streaming & content creation**
- **SE, easily.** If you’ll actually use Stream Deck actions, the side grid stops being “just MMO binds” and becomes a control surface.

## Key takeaways

- **Scimitar Elite Wireless SE** is the default pick for most people: same shell, newer 33K sensor, and much better Bluetooth endurance.
- The SE’s **Stream Deck / Virtual Stream Deck integration** is the real reason to upgrade—especially if you stream or automate workflows.
- **Scimitar Elite Wireless (original)** is the deal pick: same shape and button layout without paying for the SE’s workflow features.
- Comfort and hand feel are essentially identical, so your decision should be about **price, battery behavior, and whether Stream Deck actions matter to you**.
- If you’re not sure either Scimitar is the right “brick,” the **Corsair Darkstar Wireless RGB** and **Razer Naga V2 Pro** are the closest adjacent options with different ergonomics and ecosystems.

## FAQs

**Is the Corsair Scimitar Elite Wireless SE worth it over the original Elite Wireless?**  
Yes if prices are close. You’re paying for newer internals, far better Bluetooth endurance, and Stream Deck / Virtual Stream Deck workflows that the original doesn’t offer.

**Does 33,000 DPI matter in real MMO play?**  
Not directly—you won’t actually play at 33K DPI. The real benefit is sensor headroom and efficiency, especially on high-resolution displays and for people who want clean tracking at a wide range of DPI steps.

**Which one is better if I play MMOs and also stream?**  
The **SE**. Stream Deck actions mapped to the side grid can replace a lot of “alt-tab + click” workflow, and Virtual Stream Deck gives you multiple control layers.

**Should I use Bluetooth for raiding or competitive play?**  
Usually no. Bluetooth is fine for work and casual play, but for the lowest latency and best consistency, use Slipstream 2.4 GHz on either mouse.

**Is the original Elite Wireless “outdated”?**  
Not in the ways that matter for MMO play: the shape, button layout, and core performance are still strong. It’s “older” mostly because the SE adds modern workflow features and pushes Bluetooth battery life further.

**Are these good for small hands or fingertip grip?**  
Not really. Both are large, tall MMO shapes that suit palm or relaxed claw. If you prefer fingertip grip, they’ll likely feel bulky.

**Do I need iCUE running all the time?**  
For basic DPI/button/RGB setups, you can rely on onboard profiles after configuration. For SE-specific Stream Deck actions, you’ll want the relevant software running when you use those workflows.

## Conclusion

When you strip away the marketing, the **Corsair Scimitar Elite Wireless SE vs Scimitar Elite Wireless** decision is simple.

Both share the same big, comfortable MMO shell with a fully adjustable 12-button Key Slider side panel and tri-mode connectivity. They feel the same in hand, and they both deliver the core Scimitar experience: stable comfort for long sessions and a thumb grid that can handle an entire MMO hotbar.

The **Scimitar Elite Wireless SE** is the smarter long-term buy if you’re choosing at similar prices. You get the newest sensor generation, dramatically better Bluetooth endurance, and native Stream Deck / Virtual Stream Deck control that turns the side grid into something more than “just keybinds.”

The **Scimitar Elite Wireless (original)** still makes sense as the value Scimitar: buy it when it’s meaningfully cheaper and you simply want the Scimitar layout with iCUE macros—without paying for the SE’s workflow extras.

If pricing is close, pick the **Scimitar Elite Wireless SE**. Then use our stats, radar, and shapes tools plus the tagged MMO alternatives above to sanity-check hand fit, deals, and ecosystem—because the best MMO mouse is the one you can use for hours without thinking about it.
